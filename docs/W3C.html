World Wide Web Consortium



The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), is an organization created in 1994 and it's in charged of developing protocols and guidelines that will enable long term growth for the World Wide Web (WWW). Tim Berners-Lee is the director of the W3C, but this organization encompasses representation from around the globe. For example, the U.S. is represented by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

The W3C provides different services to it's member organization among the following:

  • Discuss issues related to the evolution of the WWW.
  • Create and maintain repositories of information, reference documents and code relating WWW protocols, services and applications
  • Create and test applications that demonstrate new types of WWW technologies

The principals of the W3C include web access for all and web access on everything. The idea is to make all the benefits of the WWW available to all people no matter what hardware, software or network infrastructure they are using.

The W3C vision is to promote rich interaction via the web, after all, the WWW was invented as communication tool to enable anyone to share information from anywhere. Also to be able to provide data and services via the web and develop trust given that in many cases the users will never meet in person but they still rely on the information provided by someone completely unknown to them.
