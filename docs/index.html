What is the Internet & How does it work?


The internet is the result of a research project from the U.S. Department of Defense called the ARPANET (Advanced Research Program Agency Network). Paul Baran was working on a communication system that could survive a nuclear attack. In order to achieve this, he had developed the idea of a distributed packet-switched network.

The internet is made up of many independently operated networks, it is fully distributed, and has no central control. The idea is that any device connected to the network should be able to connect to any other device in the network.
